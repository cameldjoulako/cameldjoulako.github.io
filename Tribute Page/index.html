Ahmadou Ahidjo

The father of independence of Cameroun

Biography

Ahidjo Ahmadou was born on August 24, 1924 in Garoua, in the north of Cameroon. He studied at Garoua and Yaoundé. He entered politics in the 1940s, in the French colony of Cameroon as a state official until 1946. Elected at the age of 23 in the Cameroonian Representative Assembly, he was re-elected in 1952 under the Cameroonian Territorial Assembly. Appointed adviser then secretary to the Assembly of the French Union in 1954, he assumed the function of Deputy Prime Minister in 1958, after the internal autonomy of Cameroon. The country obtained independence in 1960. Ahmadou became President of Cameroon and persuaded the British party to join him to form the present Federal Republic of Cameroon. He was re-elected in 1965, 1970, 1975, and 1980. In 1982, Paul Biya succeeded him following his resignation for health reasons. Six months later, his relations with the new president deteriorated. In 1984, the attempt by the supporters of the former president to piracy Paul Biya triggers a war that kills more than a thousand people. Forced to exile in 1983, Ahmadou traveled between France and Senegal, and died in Dakar on November 30, 1989. The Cameroonians keep this wound for a long time and do not celebrate the funeral of their former president. He remains, however, the founder of this country, which he led with an iron hand, showing himself hard with the opposition and the diversions of the public fortune.
